# Research paper on AI-native software development Constitution

## Core Principles

### I. Accuracy through primary source verification
All factual claims must be traceable to primary sources. Direct quotes and data must be cited accurately.

### II. Clarity for academic audience
The paper must be written for a computer science academic audience. The writing style should adhere to a Flesch-Kincaid grade level of 10-12.

### III. Reproducibility
All claims, experiments, and data analysis must be reproducible. Code and datasets should be made available where possible.

### IV. Rigor
The paper must adhere to high academic standards. Peer-reviewed sources are preferred, and a minimum of 50% of sources must be from peer-reviewed articles.

## Key Standards
- All factual claims must be traceable to sources.
- Citation format: APA style.
- Source types: minimum 50% peer-reviewed articles.
- Plagiarism check: 0% tolerance before submission.
- Writing clarity: Flesch-Kincaid grade 10-12.

## Constraints
- Word count: 5,000-7,000 words.
- Minimum 15 sources.
- Format: PDF with embedded citations.

## Success Criteria
- All claims verified against sources.
- Zero plagiarism detected.
- Passes fact-checking review.

## Governance
This constitution governs the development of the research paper. All contributors must adhere to these principles and standards. Amendments to this constitution require review and approval by all authors.
